Stephen Hawking

1942-2018

Unveiling the Mysteries of the Universe and Beyond

Stephen Hawking (1942-2018), an English physicist, defied his ALS diagnosis to become an icon of scientific inquiry. His work on black holes and Hawking radiation transformed our understanding of the cosmos. Through his bestselling book "A Brief History of Time," he made complex concepts accessible to the public, inspiring generations. Despite physical challenges, Hawking's determination and brilliance garnered prestigious awards, including the Presidential Medal of Freedom. His legacy as a theoretical physicist and science communicator endures, shaping our perception of the universe and demonstrating the power of human intellect and perseverance.

Biography

  • Born in 1942 in Oxford, England.
  • Overcame ALS to study physics at Cambridge.
  • Famous for Hawking radiation and black hole theories.
  • "A Brief History of Time" made science accessible.
  • Legacy of resilience, awards, and lasting contributions.
